I am a physics Ph.D. student at UC Berkeley studying neutrinos. The experiment I am working on is called IceCube, a cubic-kilometer particle detector buried inside the ice at the South Pole. Neutrinos are nearly massless subatomic particles that rarely interact with matter, and IceCube aims to observe high-energy cosmic neutrinos to probe violent astrophysical sources such as supernovae, gamma-ray bursts, and black hole mergers.
